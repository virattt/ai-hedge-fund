# Structural Thesis: Two-Sleeve AI Infrastructure

> *The benchmark. No substitute.*

The AI buildout is still the dominant industrial and software capex cycle of this era, but it should be owned through **two distinct sleeves**, each with a different job:

1. **Tastytrade sleeve** — the non-crypto, non-broad-index, picks-and-shovels diversification sleeve.
2. **Hyperliquid stock sleeve** — the concentrated liquid expression of AI platform winners, financial rails, and a small macro ballast.

The point is not to own "everything AI." The point is to own the **highest-conviction choke points** with minimal redundancy.

## Sleeve 1: Tastytrade

### Job

Own the durable **AI infrastructure bottlenecks** that are underrepresented in the core crypto portfolio:

- Semiconductor equipment
- EDA / design software
- Networking / switching
- Data-center power / cooling
- Select second-order power beneficiaries

### Current sleeve map

#### Core A-tier choke points

- **Equipment** — `ASML`, `AMAT`, `KLAC`, `LRCX`
- **EDA / design** — `SNPS`, `CDNS`
- **Networking / infrastructure** — `ANET`, `AVGO`
- **Power / thermal infrastructure** — `VRT`

#### Builder B-tier extensions

- **Incremental networking / silicon exposure** — `MRVL`
- **Power generation / electricity demand** — `CEG`, `EQT`
- **Storage** — `WDC`, `STX`

#### Satellite C-tier

- **Optics / photonics** — `LITE`

### What this sleeve is explicitly not

- Not broad index exposure.
- Not a duplicate of the Hyperliquid sleeve.
- Not a speculative basket of pre-scale power, quantum, battery, satellite, or crypto-adjacent names.

### Tastytrade exclusions by principle

- Prefer **durable bottlenecks** over optionality.
- Prefer **established enablers** over small-cap story stocks.
- Prefer **direct choke points** over lower-quality OEM or ETF wrappers.
- Keep storage / optics smaller than equipment / EDA / switching.

## Sleeve 2: Hyperliquid Stock Portfolio

### Job

Own the more liquid, concentrated **AI winners and financial rails** that fit the Hyperliquid stock universe without duplicating the core crypto assets (`BTC`, `ETH`, `SOL`, `HYPE`, `SUI`, `NEAR`).

### Current sleeve map

#### Core AI compute / platform winners

- **Compute** — `NVDA`
- **Foundry** — `TSM`
- **Cloud / platform** — `MSFT`, `AMZN`, `GOOGL`, `META`
- **AI software / enterprise** — `PLTR`, `ORCL`
- **Memory** — `MU`

#### Financial rails / brokerage beta

- `COIN`, `HOOD`

#### Higher-beta optionality

- `TSLA`

#### Durable consumer / edge AI

- `AAPL`

#### Small diversifiers / ballast

- **Defense** — `RTX`
- **Hard-asset ballast** — `GLD`, `SLV`

### What this sleeve is explicitly not

- Not the place for core crypto spot exposure.
- Not an ETF sleeve (`SMH`, `SPY`, `USA100` are redundant when direct winners are already owned).
- Not a home for weak execution turnarounds or low-liquidity optionality.

## Cross-Sleeve Rules

### 1. Avoid redundancy

- If a name is already captured through a better direct choke point, do not add a lower-quality adjacent name.
- Do not use ETFs where single-name conviction is already high.
- Keep the two sleeves functionally distinct:
  - **Tastytrade** = infrastructure bottlenecks
  - **Hyperliquid** = concentrated AI winners + rails + ballast

### 2. Favor directness

Prefer:

- equipment over diffuse downstream exposure
- direct semis over sector ETFs
- direct platforms over broad index proxies
- proven power beneficiaries over speculative future-build narratives

### 3. Size by durability, not just upside

Highest weights belong to:

- names with clear choke-point economics,
- mission-critical infrastructure,
- recurring software entrenchment,
- or dominant platform distribution.

Lower weights belong to:

- storage,
- optics,
- broker/market beta,
- hard-asset ballast,
- and higher-volatility optionality.

## Conviction Framework

### Tier A: core compounders / choke points

These are the names we should be willing to hold through volatility because they sit at the center of AI capex or AI distribution:

- `ASML`, `AMAT`, `KLAC`, `LRCX`, `SNPS`, `CDNS`, `ANET`, `AVGO`, `VRT`
- `NVDA`, `TSM`, `MSFT`, `META`, `AMZN`, `GOOGL`

### Tier B: builders / extensions

These are real beneficiaries, but position size should acknowledge either narrower economics, more cyclical demand, or more valuation / execution risk:

- `MRVL`, `CEG`, `EQT`, `WDC`, `STX`
- `PLTR`, `ORCL`, `MU`, `COIN`, `HOOD`, `AAPL`

### Tier C: satellites / ballast / optionality

These improve portfolio expression but should not dominate it:

- `LITE`, `TSLA`, `RTX`, `GLD`, `SLV`

## Regime Overlay

> *Precision. No noise. Just the signal.*

### Trending-bull

- Full size in Tier A.
- Let Tier B run, especially platform and broker beta.
- Keep ballast small.

### Mean-reverting / indecisive

- Keep core choke points and platform monopolies.
- Trim higher-beta extensions and optionality.
- Maintain some ballast.

### Capitulation / liquidity stress

- Reduce everything except the most durable core names.
- Favor cash, ballast, and only the strongest choke points / platform monopolies.
- Avoid adding new speculative exposure.

## Decision Rules

When evaluating a ticker, ask in this order:

1. **Which sleeve does this belong in?**
2. **Is it a direct choke point, a platform winner, a second-order beneficiary, or just thematic clutter?**
3. **Does it duplicate an exposure we already own better elsewhere?**
4. **Is the durability strong enough for Tier A/B, or is this only a satellite?**
5. **What should regime do to the size, not the thesis?**

Use this thesis to keep the portfolio concentrated, non-redundant, and structurally aligned with the AI buildout rather than drifting into generic quality-stock ownership.
