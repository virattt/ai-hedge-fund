# Structural Thesis: AI Infrastructure

The AI infrastructure supply chain represents the largest capital deployment cycle since postwar industrial expansion. Every analysis and sizing decision should be contextualized against this thesis.

## Layers (Supply Chain)

1. **Chip designers** — NVDA, AAPL (and other design houses). Highest margin, most visible; demand is regime-sensitive.
2. **Foundry** — TSM. Bottleneck; durable through cycles; capacity and geopolitics matter.
3. **Equipment** — AMAT, BESI, ASML, LRCX, KLAC. Long-duration capex; orders lead cycles; high durability.
4. **EDA** — SNPS, CDNS. Critical enablers; sticky; growth tied to design activity.
5. **Power & infrastructure** — BE, CRWV, CORZ (and power/ cooling enablers). Physical constraints of AI buildout; thematic and strategic sizing.

## Conviction Tiers (Target Allocation Bands)

- **Core** — 50–60% of book. Highest-conviction, durable names (e.g. equipment, foundry, key design).
- **Growth** — 20–25%. Secular growers with clear AI linkage; regime-sensitive sizing.
- **Thematic** — 10–15%. Emerging themes (e.g. power, edge, sovereign).
- **Strategic** — 5–10%. Optionality and catalysts; smaller size.
- **Hedge** — Small. Defensive or counter-thesis positions; used for risk-off or correlation hedge.

## Sizing Rules

- **Regime** determines *size*: trending-bull → full size; mean-reverting → reduce; capitulation → minimal or hedge-only.
- **Layer** determines *durability*: equipment/EDA/foundry → hold through drawdowns; growth/design → trim on regime shift.
- **Catalyst** determines *timing*: earnings, product, policy — use for entry/exit, not for tier assignment.

## Regime Overlay

- **Trending-bull** — Full position sizes; growth and thematic can run.
- **Mean-reverting** — Reduce growth/thematic; keep core; tighten stops.
- **Capitulation** — Core only at reduced size; add hedges; avoid new growth/thematic.

Use this thesis to place each ticker in a layer and tier, and to align recommendations with regime and catalyst.
